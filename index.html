Who Is Hermon Taye?

My name is Hermon Taye and art has been an integral part of my life for as long as I can remember. Growing up, I was always drawn to creative expression, whether through drawing, crafting, or simply experimenting with different materials. What really pulled me toward art was how it allows people to express emotions and ideas without needing words. I was always fascinated by how colors, shapes, and designs could tell a story or make people feel something. Over time, this love for creating led me to study visual communications at San Francisco State University, where I’m now in my third year.

In addition to digital art and drawing, I also enjoy sewing and making things by hand. During the pandemic, I decided to teach myself how to sew and I'm so grateful for that decision. I’ve taken old clothes I don’t wear anymore and turned them into bags, which has been an incredibly fun and rewarding way to repurpose materials. I love the process of taking something unused and transforming it into something new. Whether I’m working digitally or with fabric, I enjoy the challenge of bringing ideas to life.

Right now, I’m excited to learn more about digital art and all the possibilities it offers. I’m still getting familiar with it, but from what I know so far, it seems like such a cool and creative field with so many opportunities. After college, I hope to use my art in a way that combines creativity with practical design, whether that’s in branding, illustration, or something else in visual communications.
